The problem

Households with solar sell back to the grid at $0.10/kWh while neighbours buy at $0.50/kWh. The utility captures a 40-cent spread for energy that could travel 10 metres over a fence.

The solution

SLIM trains 4 autonomous agents — Solar, Wind, EV/V2G, Standard — to trade energy peer-to-peer using a Uniform Price Double Auction and a GATv2 Graph Neural Network policy.

The breakthrough

The initial model collapsed to an all-seller Nash equilibrium (zero buyers, zero P2P). Three targeted fixes broke this trap, achieving 992.77 kWh — 1363% above the auction baseline.

Why more agents trade more

Larger networks have higher prosumer diversity — solar peaks at midday, wind at night, EV returns at 17:00. More diverse profiles mean higher probability of finding a complementary buyer for every seller.

This is the network liquidity effect — the core finding of the scalability experiments. Profit improves monotonically from -$38.05 to -$33.88 as N grows to 10.

Tier 1 — projection-based (AutonomousGuard)
Deterministic, stateless. Three layers: jitter clipping (slew rate limiting), FeasibilityFilter (SoC bounds, surplus limits), SafetySupervisor (hard veto with zero-action fallback). Guarantees zero violations every timestep regardless of policy quality.
hard guarantee
Tier 2 — Lagrangian primal-dual (LagrangianSafetyLayer)
Soft constraints via reward shaping. Three Lagrange multipliers updated after each episode via gradient ascent. Teaches the policy where the safety boundary is — reducing hard-clip interventions over training.
